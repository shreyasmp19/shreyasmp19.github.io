How to use the toolbar?

The symbol toolbar in the Latex Editor provides a convenient way to insert various mathematical symbols and operators into your documents. The toolbar is organized into different categories, each containing a set of related symbols. Below is a detailed explanation of each toolbar category and its functionality:

  • Accents

    The "Accents" category allows you to apply accents to your symbols, enhancing their appearance. Choose from a variety of accents to add the perfect touch to your mathematical expressions.

  • Delimiters

    In the "Delimiters" category, you'll find a collection of delimiters to encapsulate and structure your equations. Delimiters include parentheses, brackets, braces, and more.

  • Environments

    The "Environments" category provides a selection of environments for specialized formatting. Choose the environment that suits your needs, such as aligning equations or creating matrices.

  • Logic & Set Theory

    For logical expressions and set theory, the "Logic & Set Theory" category offers a range of symbols to represent operations like intersection, union, and logical connectives.

  • Letters and Unicode

    In the "Letters and Unicode" category, you can easily insert letters and special Unicode characters into your equations. This is particularly useful for incorporating specific symbols and alphabets.

  • Big Operators

    For large mathematical operators, the "Big Operators" category provides symbols like summation and integration signs. Easily incorporate these operators for comprehensive mathematical expressions.

  • Binary Operators

    The "Binary Operators" category includes symbols for binary operations, such as plus, minus, multiplication, and division. Use these operators to build fundamental mathematical expressions..

  • Fractions Operators

    Create fractions effortlessly with the "Fractions Operators" category. Choose from a variety of fraction symbols to represent mathematical ratios in your equations.

  • Math Operators

    The "Maths Operators" category offers a diverse set of mathematical operators for a wide range of calculations. From square roots to mathematical functions, find the operators you need.

  • Relational Operators

    In the "Relations" category, discover symbols for expressing relationships between mathematical elements. Include symbols like equal, not equal, less than, and greater than.

  • Negated Relational Operators

    The "Negated Relations" category provides negated versions of relation symbols, allowing you to express negations within mathematical statements.

  • Arrows

    For indicating direction or movement, the "Arrows" category offers a selection of arrow symbols. Use these symbols to illustrate transitions or relationships in your equations.

  • Extended Arrows

    Explore extended arrow options in the "Ext Arrows" category. Choose from an array of extended arrows to convey specific meanings within your mathematical expressions.

Troubleshooting

Issue: Equation not rendering correctly in the live preview

Solution: Ensure that your LaTeX code is valid. Check for syntax errors, unmatched braces, or other issues. If the problem persists, try refreshing the page.

Issue: Exported image is blank or incomplete.

Solution: Verify that your equation is visible in the live preview before exporting. If the issue persists, try exporting the equation in a different image format.

Issue: Symbols are not displaying in the toolbar

Solution: Ensure that your internet connection is stable. If the issue persists, try reloading the page or using a different browser.
